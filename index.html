How to play

  • Count the neighbors: each number tells how many light tiles sit in the 3×3 block centered on it — including the numbered tile itself.
  • 9 = the whole block is light. 0 = the whole block is dark.
  • Clues only count tiles inside their own map — the odd-shaped territories with thick borders.
  • Numbers fade once satisfied; they turn red if contradicted.
  • Gold plots are sudoku, stitched right into the canvas: click a tile and type a digit (or click to cycle). Every row, column and outlined shape needs each number exactly once.
  • Teal patches are picross: hover near one and its run clues appear. Each number is a run of consecutive light tiles, in order, in that row or column. Stitch them with the usual left/right click — the answer is the painting itself.
  • Finish a map, plot or patch and the painting beneath is revealed.
  • Difficulty (toolbar) scales everything — easier tiers keep more clues and more free 0/9 moves, give sudoku plots extra digits and tamer shapes, and pre-stitch a head start into picross patches. Your stitches and digits always survive the switch.
  • Very Easy–Medium need only neighbor-counting. Hard and Very Hard add Advanced Deductions (comparing overlapping clues) — but guessing is never required on any tier. Stuck? Press H for a hint.

Advanced Deductions

On Hard and up you'll meet spots where no single clue alone fixes a tile. Compare two adjacent clues instead — especially ones 3 apart in value.

3
6

■ forced dark  ·  ■ forced light  ·  the four middle tiles are shared by both clues

The 6 and 3 overlap on the middle tiles. The 6 needs three more lights than the 3, and there are exactly three tiles only the 6 can see — so those must all be light, and the three only the 3 sees must all be dark. Forced — never a guess. Counting takes over again from there.

If in doubt, use a hint (H). Guessing is never required.
